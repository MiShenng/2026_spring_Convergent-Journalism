The camera returns to the Mogao Caves. The Apsaras on the wall and the Apsaras on the screen share one frame — explaining each other, not replacing each other.

If digitisation only stores the image and never lets it be used, the Apsaras stays in the archive. If circulation loses its boundaries, it wears down into ornament with no meaning. Real revival needs both to happen at once — more eyes, and the cultural thread kept alongside them.
